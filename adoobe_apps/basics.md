#              Raster vs Vector (CRITICAL CONCEPT)

Raster (Photoshop)

Made of pixels
Quality depends on resolution
Enlarging causes blur / pixelation
Examples: Photos, scanned images, textures

Vector (Illustrator)

Made of paths & mathematical shapes
Infinitely scalable
No quality loss at any size

Examples: Logos, icons, illustrations   


Examples: Logos, icons, illustrations

| ----------- | ------ | ----------------------- |

# 1. Composition Theory

## 1. Clear Definition

### Why it matters

- Good composition tells the viewer where to look.
- Bad composition makes the image confusing.

### How to use it

- Photography ‚Üí Move subject, change angle, clean background.
- Product editing ‚Üí Keep product as clear hero.
- Social media ‚Üí Keep subject visible even in small screen.
- Cinematic video ‚Üí Use framing to show emotion (wide = lonely, close = intense).

### Editing tips

- Crop tighter in Lightroom.
- Remove distractions in Photoshop.
- Reframe shots in Premiere/Resolve.

### Beginner mistake

- Keeping everything in the frame. Learn to remove.

### Pro tip

- Before shooting or editing, ask:
- ‚ÄúWhat is the main focus here?‚Äù
  
---------------------------------------------

## 2. Rule of Thirds

### What it is

- Divide the frame into 9 boxes.
- Place important things on the lines or where lines cross.

### Why it matters

- It makes images feel natural and dynamic instead of boring center placement.

### How to use it

- Portrait ‚Üí Eyes on top line.
- Landscape ‚Üí Horizon on top or bottom third.
- Product ‚Üí Slightly off center looks more premium.
- Video ‚Üí Subject off center adds movement feeling.

### Editing tip

- Turn on grid while cropping.

### Beginner mistake

- Forcing everything to the grid. It‚Äôs a guide, not a rule.

---------------------------------------------

## 3. Leading Lines

### What it is

- Lines that guide the viewer‚Äôs eye to the subject.

### Why it matters

- Eyes naturally follow lines.

### How to use it

- Roads, stairs, rails in photography.
- Table edges in product shots.
- Arrows or layout direction in social posts.
- Hallways or light beams in cinematic shots.

### Editing tip

- Increase contrast slightly to make lines clearer.

### Beginner mistake

- Lines leading outside the frame instead of toward subject.

---------------------------------------------

## 4. Visual Balance

### What it is

- Making the image feel stable.

### Why it matters

- If one side feels heavier, image feels uncomfortable.

### How to use it

- Symmetry for clean, powerful look.
- Asymmetry for dynamic look.
- Balance big subject with empty space.

### Editing tip

- Crop and adjust position until frame feels stable.

### Beginner mistake

- Too much empty space on one side without reason.

---------------------------------------------

## 5. Negative Space

### What it is

- The empty area around the main subject.

### Why it matters

- Makes image clean and premium.
- Luxury brands use this a lot.

### How to use it

- Leave sky or wall space around subject.
- Don‚Äôt fill every part of frame.
- Great for text placement in ads.

### Editing tip

- Blur background in Lightroom/Photoshop.
- Use gradient to darken empty areas.

### Beginner mistake

- Filling every inch of the frame.

---------------------------------------------

## 6. Depth & Layering

### What it is

- Depth means making the image feel 3D, not flat.
- Layering = foreground, middle, background.

### Why it matters

- Flat images look cheap.
- Depth makes images cinematic and professional.

### How to use it

- Photography ‚Üí Add something in foreground (blurred leaves, wall edge).
- Product ‚Üí Slight shadow under product creates depth.
- Social ‚Üí Background blur makes subject pop.
- Cinematic ‚Üí Shoot through objects for dramatic feel.

### Editing tip

- Lightroom ‚Üí Use Radial Mask to blur background slightly.
- Photoshop ‚Üí Add soft shadow under subject.
- Video ‚Üí Use depth blur or light falloff.

### Beginner mistake

- Everything sharp and flat.

### Pro tip

- Foreground blur + sharp subject = instant cinematic look.

---------------------------------------------

## 7. Perspective

### What it is

- Perspective is the angle you shoot from.

### Why it matters

Angle changes power and emotion.

- Low angle ‚Üí Powerful
- High angle ‚Üí Weak or small
- Eye level ‚Üí Neutral

### How to use it

- Product ‚Üí Shoot from slightly above for clean look.
- Portrait ‚Üí Eye level for connection.
- Cinematic ‚Üí Low angle for drama, high angle for isolation.

### Editing tip

- Use transform tool to fix distorted lines.

### Beginner mistake

- Shooting everything from eye level.

### Pro tip

- Move your body, not just your camera.

---------------------------------------------

## 8. Color Theory

### What it is

- Color theory is how colors work together.

### Basic ideas

- Warm colors ‚Üí Energy (red, orange, yellow)
- Cool colors ‚Üí Calm (blue, green)
- Complementary colors ‚Üí Opposite on color wheel (strong contrast)

### Why it matters

- Color controls emotion instantly.

### How to use it

- Product ‚Üí Use brand colors.
- Social ‚Üí Use 2‚Äì3 colors max.
- Cinematic ‚Üí Use color grading to create mood.

### Editing tip

- Lightroom ‚Üí Adjust Temperature & Color Grading panel.
- Photoshop ‚Üí Use Selective Color or Gradient Map.
- Video ‚Üí Use color wheels for shadows/midtones/highlights.

### Beginner mistake

- Over-saturating colors.

### Pro tip

- Reduce saturation slightly for more professional look.

---------------------------------------------

## 9. Contrast Control

### What it is

- Contrast = difference between light and dark.

### Why it matters

- High contrast ‚Üí Dramatic, bold.
- Low contrast ‚Üí Soft, calm.

### How to use it

- Photography ‚Üí Add contrast for punch.
- Product ‚Üí Enough contrast to show shape clearly.
- Social ‚Üí Higher contrast grabs attention.
- Cinematic ‚Üí Control contrast for mood.

### Editing tip

- Use contrast slider carefully.
- Use tone curve for more control.

### Beginner mistake

- Too much contrast = crushed blacks.

### Pro tip

- Add contrast in shadows for depth, not just overall.

---------------------------------------------

## 10. Light Direction

### What it is

- Light direction = Where the light is coming from.

### Why it matters

- Light direction creates mood and shape.

### How to use it

Light direction creates shape and drama.

- Front light ‚Üí Flat
- Side light ‚Üí Depth
- Back light ‚Üí Glow / cinematic

### How to use it

- Photography ‚Üí Use window light from side.
- Product ‚Üí Side light shows texture.
- Social ‚Üí Avoid harsh overhead light.
- Cinematic ‚Üí Backlight for emotional scenes.

### Editing tip

- Use dodge & burn to enhance light direction.

### Beginner mistake

- Using only front light.

### Pro tip

- Side light makes products look premium.

---------------------------------------------

## 11. Shadow Realism

### What it is

- Shadows should look natural, not fake.

### Why it matters

- Fake shadows instantly look edited and cheap.

### How to use it

- Product ‚Üí Add soft shadow under product.
- Photography ‚Üí Keep natural shadow direction.
- Social ‚Üí Avoid floating objects.
- Cinematic ‚Üí Hard shadows for drama.

### Editing tip

Photoshop ‚Üí Multiply layer + blur for natural shadow.

### Beginner mistake

Shadow too dark or wrong direction.

### Pro tip

Shadows are never pure black. Keep them slightly soft.

---------------------------------------------

## 12. Visual Hierarchy

### What it is

- Order in which people see things.

- Big ‚Üí Medium ‚Üí Small.

### Why it matters

- If everything screams, nothing stands out.

### How to use it

- Product ‚Üí Product first, text second.
- Social ‚Üí Headline big, details small.
- Cinematic ‚Üí Subject brighter than background.

### Editing tip

- Increase brightness or contrast on main subject.

### Beginner mistake

Same font size everywhere.

### Pro tip

One clear focal point per frame.

---------------------------------------------

- How text is designed and placed.

### Why it matters

- Bad typography kills professional look.

### How to use it

- Use 1‚Äì2 fonts max.
